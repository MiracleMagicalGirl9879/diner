Sinaing na Tulingan

Sinaing na Tulingan is a dish from Batangas because the Batangueños are fond of making dishes from fish because of it being abundant in the province. The dish is cooked slowly with a small amount of water which is the definition of sinaing and it is often cooked using tulingan which is a type of fish. Often cooked in a clay pot and the fish is wrapped in banana leave

Sinaing na Tulingan vs. Paksiw na Isda

Sinaing na tulingan doesn't use vinegar much unlike paksiw na isda and it is slow cooked and often uses a clay pot for it to be made and there is a difference between the ingredients. Sinaing na Tulingan contains banana leaves, dried kamias, water, salt and pork fat while paksiw na isda contains garlic, ginger, vinegar and other ingredients.

Sinaing na Tulingan Batangueño Version

Course Type: Main Dish
Cuisine: Filipino Cuisine
Keyword:Tulingan
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 4 hours
Total Time: 4 hours and 30 minutes
Serving: One Sinaing na Tulingan fish per saving
Calories: 132 calories per saving
Author: Yajeni Yhienze M. Baguisi

Ingredients

  • 4 medium sized tulingan fish
  • 10 pieces ried kamias
  • 4-6 cups of water
  • 5 tablespoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Banana leaves for wrapping the fish

Instructions

  1. Clean the fish make sure to remove the innards, gills and tail from the fish
  2. Make incisions and make abou 4 slanting cuts for the fish to absorb more flavor
  3. Soak the tulingan to make sure that no blood is left and the fish is fully clean for the perfect braising
  4. Salt the fish and make sure to salt the insides
  5. Prepare the kamias
  6. Arrange the fish and make sure to wrap it in banana leaves then arranged them ontop of the kamias
  7. Add Water and Cook for 4-5 hours and add water as needed to keep the fish covered
  8. Serve the dish and enjoy with hot steaming rice
